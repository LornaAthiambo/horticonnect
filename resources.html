About Horticulture

Horticulture is the science and art of growing fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, herbs, sprouts, mushrooms, algae, and non-food crops such as grass and flowers. It encompasses various aspects including plant propagation, breeding, and production.

The primary goal of horticulture is to produce high-quality plants and crops for consumption, aesthetic enjoyment, and other uses. This field involves understanding plant biology, soil science, pest management, and sustainable practices to ensure the health and productivity of plants.

Horticulturists work in diverse settings such as gardens, greenhouses, nurseries, and farms, contributing to food security, environmental sustainability, and landscape enhancement. For more information about Horticulture, visit HortiConnect.
